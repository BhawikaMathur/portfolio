Khatu Shyam Ji Temple (Shekhawati, Rajasthan)

Introduction

Khatu Shyam Ji Temple is a famous Hindu temple located in Khatu village in the Sikar district of Rajasthan, India. It is dedicated to Lord Shyam, who is believed to be the manifestation of Barbarika, the grandson of Bhima from the Mahabharata.

Location

The temple is situated in Khatu village, Sikar district, in the Shekhawati region of Rajasthan. It is approximately 80 km from Jaipur and well connected by road and rail.

History

The history of Khatu Shyam Ji is rooted in the Mahabharata. Barbarika, the son of Ghatotkacha and grandson of Bhima, was a great warrior blessed with three powerful arrows.

Before the Kurukshetra war, Lord Krishna tested Barbarika’s power and devotion. Barbarika had vowed to support the weaker side in battle, which could have prolonged the war indefinitely. To prevent this, Lord Krishna asked for his head as a donation (daan).

Barbarika sacrificed his head willingly. Impressed by his devotion, Lord Krishna granted him a boon that he would be worshipped in the Kaliyuga by the name Shyam.

Discovery of the Idol

It is believed that Barbarika’s head was buried in Khatu village. Many centuries later, the idol was discovered when a cow started giving milk at a specific spot. The place was excavated and the sacred head was found.

Construction of the Temple

The temple was originally built in 1027 AD by Roopsingh Chauhan and his wife Narmada Kanwar. Later, it was renovated by Diwan Abhay Singh in the 18th century.

Architecture

The temple is constructed using white marble and features intricate carvings. The sanctum houses the idol of Shyam Baba, decorated beautifully with flowers and jewelry.

Deity (Shyam Baba)

Shyam Baba is worshipped as a form of Lord Krishna. Devotees believe that he fulfills wishes and protects them from difficulties. He is also called "Haare Ka Sahara" (supporter of the defeated).

Rituals & Aarti

  • Mangala Aarti (morning)
  • Bhog Aarti (afternoon)
  • Sandhya Aarti (evening)
  • Shayan Aarti (night)

Festivals

The most important festival is the Phalgun Mela, held in February–March. Lakhs of devotees visit the temple during this time.

  • Phalgun Mela
  • Janmashtami
  • Ekadashi celebrations

Phalgun Mela

This grand fair is celebrated on the Ekadashi and Dwadashi of the Phalgun month. Devotees gather in huge numbers and sing bhajans in praise of Shyam Baba.

Shyam Kund

Shyam Kund is a sacred pond near the temple. It is believed to be the place where Barbarika’s head was found. Devotees take a holy dip here.

Beliefs & Significance

Devotees believe that Khatu Shyam Ji fulfills all wishes and removes sorrows. He is considered especially powerful in Kaliyuga.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is during winter (October to March) and during the Phalgun Mela for experiencing grand celebrations.

Conclusion

Khatu Shyam Ji Temple is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in Rajasthan. With deep mythological roots, spiritual significance, and vibrant festivals, it attracts millions of devotees every year.
