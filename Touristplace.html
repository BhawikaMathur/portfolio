Tourist Places in Shekhawati

Shekhawati, often called the "Open Air Art Gallery" of Rajasthan, comprises the Sikar, Jhunjhunu, and Churu districts, renowned for their immense concentration of 18th-20th century painted havelis. Key tourist spots include Mandawa’s fort and havelis, Nawalgarh’s Poddar Haveli Museum, Fatehpur’s frescos, and Jhunjhunu's Khetri Mahal. The region boasts intricate mural paintings, traditional stepwells, and stunning frescoes, offering a unique, artistic, and historical travel experience.

Explore Top Tourist Places

Mandawa – The Open Air Art Gallery of Rajasthan

Mandawa is one of the most famous tourist destinations in the Shekhawati region of Rajasthan. Founded in the 18th century, the town is widely known for its beautifully painted havelis decorated with colorful frescoes depicting mythological stories, royal lifestyles, and European influences. The magnificent Mandawa Fort, now a heritage hotel, stands as the main attraction of the town. Visitors can also explore famous havelis like Jhunjhunwala Haveli and Goenka Chhatri, which showcase the artistic and cultural richness of Rajasthan.

Nawalgarh – The Golden City of Magnificent Havelis

Nawalgarh, founded in 1737 by Thakur Nawal Singh, is one of the most important towns in the Shekhawati region. It is known for its large number of beautifully decorated havelis that display detailed fresco paintings. The Poddar Haveli Museum is a major attraction where visitors can learn about the history and art of the region. Other popular sites include Morarka Haveli, Aath Haveli Complex, and Roop Niwas Palace. The town’s stunning artwork and rich heritage make it a must-visit destination for tourists.

Fatehpur – The Cultural Heart of Shekhawati

Fatehpur, founded in the 16th century, is considered the cultural center of the Shekhawati region. The town is famous for its impressive havelis decorated with intricate fresco paintings that reflect Indian mythology, historical events, and local traditions. One of the most notable attractions is the Nadine Le Prince Haveli, a restored heritage building showcasing traditional Shekhawati art. Visitors can also explore Singhania Haveli, Dwarkadheesh Temple, and historic stepwells that highlight the architectural beauty of the town.

Jhunjhunu – The Historic Land of Brave Warriors

Jhunjhunu is an important historical town in the Shekhawati region and is known for its grand temples, forts, and painted havelis. The town reflects the rich cultural heritage and bravery of Rajasthan’s warrior traditions. One of the most famous landmarks is the Rani Sati Temple, a major pilgrimage site visited by thousands of devotees. Another unique structure is Khetri Mahal, also called the “Wind Palace,” known for its open design that allows natural air flow throughout the building.

Dundlod – The Royal Town of Forts and Fresco Havelis

Dundlod is a charming heritage town located in the heart of the Shekhawati region. It is best known for the impressive Dundlod Fort, built in the 18th century, which has now been converted into a heritage hotel. The town is also famous for its beautifully painted havelis and artistic chhatris that showcase traditional Rajasthani architecture. Dundlod is also well known for Marwari horse breeding, and visitors can enjoy horse safaris that offer a unique way to explore the historic surroundings.

Mukundgarh – The Heritage Town of Artistic Havelis

Mukundgarh is a peaceful and historic town famous for its stunning fresco-painted havelis and traditional Rajasthani architecture. Established in the 18th century, the town features beautiful mansions such as the Kanoria Haveli and Ganeriwala Haveli, known for their detailed wall paintings and artistic designs. The Mukundgarh Fort, now a heritage hotel, is another important attraction that reflects the royal history of the region. Visitors can enjoy exploring the narrow streets filled with colorful art and cultural heritage.
